Word-wise model

This model is a machine learning classifer trained on 21,678 single word names which were extracted from full names. Each name was assigned its gender based on the full name it comes from.

First the input name is divided into words and passed into the model. The model outputs the detected gender of the word and also how confident it is. The final verdict is calculated letting the gender values and confidences cancel each other out. For example, a name with two words where one word is low confidence male and the other is medium confidence female will be considered female.

Full name model

This model is a machine learning model trained on 5,44,838 bengali names. This model will work best with full names but does also work with single words. Currently it removes all special characters like "." or "-".

The full name model is better at identifying the effect of words on entire name. For example, "rahman" is evidently present in both female and male names. The word-wise model sets its gender as male or female based on its frequency. So during calculation "rahman" affects the final outcome equally as other words in the name. On the other hand this model will let the other words in the name affect the final verdict more than this word since its not conclusive evidence.
